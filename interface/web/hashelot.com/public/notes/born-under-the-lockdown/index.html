Lockdown Ethereum

You know those projects you keep postponing as you wait for the right moment to tackle them, but then a global pandemic hits the whole planet and you are faced with the choice of dedicating your newly acquired spare time doing chores around the house or pretending you are studying for an important project you had been postponing (waiting for the right time to develop it) and you choose wisely?

This is one of those projects.

I am from one of the first countries hit by Covid-19, Italy, in one of the hardest clobbered regions, Lombardy, but so far I managed to dodge the viral bullet out of carefulness, luck, or, who knows, simple mercy by the reptilian overlords that grant us the new opium of the people, social networks1.

Hashelot is an experiment, a gateway to the jungle of decentralized ideas, an opportunity to study existing projects and play with smart contracts along the way, with Ethereum as a starting point.

The choice of kicking off this journey with Vitalik’s offspring as a travel mate is neither a statement against the behemoth that is Bitcoin (although given the chance to talk about it I’d like to point out some its merits and flaws in a future note), nor a complete and blind endorsement of Ethereum.

It is just a fun thing to do right now and it might propel the research on current designs and maybe the development of novel ones.

Speaking of propelling forces, as I publish this first note on this new website I introduce you, the readers, to Rings, my first smart contract, a stacking game written in Solidity and deployed on the Ethereum main network. I hope you’ll enjoy playing with it!
